# Research Dossier: design-tool-handoff-jp

Research date: 2026-05-14
Output: `design-tool-handoff-jp`

## Summary

Design handoff should stay memo/checklist-only. The workflow can prepare instructions
for Canva/Figma/Slides/designers, but it must not assert copyright, trademark,
license, likeness, or advertising-platform clearance.

## Sources consulted

- Agency for Cultural Affairs, [AIと著作権](https://www.bunka.go.jp/seisaku/chosakuken/aiandcopyright.html?hl=ja-JP), checked 2026-05-14.
- JPO, [意匠制度の概要](https://www.jpo.go.jp/system/design/gaiyo/seidogaiyo/torokugaiyo/index.html), checked 2026-05-14.
- Agency for Cultural Affairs, [著作権登録制度](https://www.bunka.go.jp/seisaku/chosakuken/seidokaisetsu/toroku_seido/index.html), checked 2026-05-14.

## Safe flags

`memo_only`, `checklist_only`, `rights_review_required`, `license_unconfirmed`,
`no_auto_publish`, `owner_review_required`, `upstream_alias_allowed`.

## Forbidden claims

- Commercial use is fully cleared.
- Copyright was transferred.
- Trademark registration or use is safe.
- Canva or platform license is confirmed.
- Ad/social review is guaranteed.

## Memo-only implications

Use asset and license checklists. If handing to Canva or an upstream workflow,
state that the owner must confirm rights and tool terms before publishing.

## Refresh trigger

Refresh when copyright, trademark, design-right, AI asset, or platform-license
guidance changes.
