# Research Dossier: invoice-chase-jp

Research date: 2026-05-14
Output: `invoice-chase-jp`
Scope: Japanese invoice reminder packet and qualified-invoice boundary

## Summary

`invoice-chase-jp` should be memo-only in v0. It may extract invoice fields,
flag missing data, draft a payment reminder for owner approval, and prepare
questions for an accountant or tax accountant. It must not generate
tool-specific import CSVs, issue invoices, decide qualified-invoice validity,
decide input-tax-credit availability, or send reminders.

## Fields to recognize

- issuer name.
- qualified invoice registration number candidate, `T` plus 13 digits.
- transaction date or covered period.
- goods / service description.
- reduced-tax-rate marker, if any.
- tax-rate-separated subtotal or total.
- applied tax rate.
- consumption tax amount by rate.
- buyer name, except where simplified invoice treatment may apply.
- invoice number, issue date, due date, paid amount, unpaid amount.

## Safe flags

- field missing, unreadable, or inconsistent.
- registration-number format mismatch.
- official registration check not performed or not timestamped.
- arithmetic mismatch candidate.
- reduced-tax-rate marker missing where rates appear mixed.
- buyer name missing, with simplified-invoice applicability left for review.
- exempt-business / no-registration-number case requiring professional review,
  not immediate classification as a defect. Transitional measures may apply and
  must be mentioned as a review point rather than decided by the workflow.

## Forbidden claims

- the document is a valid qualified invoice.
- input tax credit is available.
- tax treatment is correct.
- a qualified professional checked the output.
- the document can be used as-is for filing, submission, or compliance.
- the assistant will contact the customer or issue a revised invoice.
- an exempt business must register or cut its price.

## Memo-only implications

Use Markdown memo only. Avoid tool-specific CSV/import files because those can
look like completed accounting work and blur responsibility for registration
verification, rounding, transition rules, simplified invoice eligibility, and
professional review.

Recommended sections:

- `review_memo`
- `extracted_fields`
- `flags`
- `needs_human_review`
- `draft_reminder`
- `source_links`
- `checked_at`
- `not_a_tax_opinion`

## SubAgent counterproof

SubAgent review on 2026-05-14 challenged:

- applied tax rate must be explicit, not only tax amount.
- simplified invoice eligibility is fact-specific.
- rounding must not be judged as tax correctness.
- public-site scraping and API terms must be respected.
- exempt-business and transition-rule cases must not be treated as defects by
  default.

These points are reflected in the output contract.
