# Research Dossier: smb-onboard-jp

Research date: 2026-05-14
Output: `smb-onboard-jp`

## Summary

The onboarding workflow is the lowest-risk first upstream slice because it does
not calculate taxes, make legal judgments, submit filings, send messages, or
write to external tools. It only gathers owner context, identifies available
data sources, and recommends a safe first workflow.

## Safe flags

`memo_only`, `one_source_start`, `no_live_connector_required`,
`professional_questions_only`, `no_external_write`, `data_minimization`.

## Forbidden claims

- Onboarding completed legal, tax, labor, accounting, HR, or administrative
  compliance.
- The workflow can submit filings, represent the owner, or make final
  determinations.
- Tool connections are required before any useful result.

## Memo-only implications

Onboarding should produce a business context draft, available-data map, first
safe workflow recommendation, missing-data list, and professional handoff
questions as sections inside a memo only.

## Prior tool pattern

The upstream Small Business onboarding flow asks owners to connect tools, run a
first recipe, capture business context, and set a weekly rhythm. The Japan-local
starter keeps the same shape but treats CSV/PDF/pasted context as first-class
starting points.

## Output impact

The output contract requires:

- one-source startup.
- explicit missing-data notes.
- approval boundary.
- no professional conclusion.
- no external write.

## Negative scope

- no filing.
- no final tax / legal / labor / accounting decision.
- no professional-title display.
- no external send.
- no live connector requirement.
