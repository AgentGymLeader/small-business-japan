# Research Dossier: ticket-deflector-jp

Research date: 2026-05-14
Output: `ticket-deflector-jp`

## Summary

Complaint-response workflows can draft wording and identify missing facts. They
must not decide refunds, consumer rights, chargeback outcomes, legal liability,
or send messages.

## Sources consulted

- CAA, [特定商取引法ガイド 通信販売](https://www.no-trouble.caa.go.jp/what/mailorder/?r=freelancer), checked 2026-05-14.
- CAA, [通信販売広告Q&A](https://www.no-trouble.caa.go.jp/qa/advertising.html), checked 2026-05-14.
- PPC, [個人情報保護法ガイドライン 通則編](https://www.ppc.go.jp/personalinfo/legal/guidelines_tsusoku/), checked 2026-05-14.

## Safe flags

`draft_reply_requires_human_review`, `refund_policy_lookup_required`,
`chargeback_escalate`, `consumer_rights_neutral`, `no_auto_send`.

## Forbidden claims

- Refund is definitively unavailable.
- Chargeback is impossible or illegal.
- Cooling-off treatment is conclusively decided.
- The reply resolves the dispute.

## Memo-only implications

Draft replies should be calm and factual. Put terms, refund, cancellation, and
platform-dispute points into a review checklist.

## Refresh trigger

Refresh when consumer, ecommerce, payment-dispute, or APPI guidance changes.
